Supplementary Soft robot videos

A Soft Robot "Finger"

The cable driven soft robot from the example in Chapter 4. A cable runs through the middle of each of the wedges of the robot.

The Cable-Driven Beam

The cable driven soft robot from Experiment 4.3.2 in Chapter 4. A cable runs through the robot and is attached in each end. Only a small deformation is produced, as the cable is only pulled 6.5 mm.

Inflating a Hollow Cuboid

The pressure-driven robot from Experiment 5.3.1 in Chapter 5. A single air-chamber corresponding to the surface of the cavity in the robot is inflated, increasing the original pressure by a factor of 10 percent.

Deflating a Hollow Cuboid

The pressure-driven robot from Experiment 5.3.2 in Chapter 5. A single air-chamber corresponding to the surface of the cavity in the robot is deflated, decreasing the original pressure by a factor of 2.5 percent.

The Hammerbot

The Hammerbot from Chapter 6. The robot is equipped with two cables, each with a length of 100 mm.

The Spinebot

The Spinebot from Chapter 6. The robot is equipped with two cables, each with a length of 70.5 mm.

The Bubblefinger Robot (Coarse)

The "coarse-mesh" pressure-driven robot from Experiment 6.3.4 in Chapter 6. The internal pressure is increased by a factor of roughly 2.5.

The Bubblefinger Robot (Fine)

The "fine-mesh" pressure-driven robot from Experiment 6.3.4 in Chapter 6. The internal pressure is increased by a factor of roughly 2.5.

The Deep Bubble robot

The Deep Bubble robot from chapter 6. The robot has three inflatable air-chambers.

The Steepest Descent Controller

The Hammerbot minimising the distance between the green and red cross using steepest descent. This is one of the cases from Experiment 7.3.1 in Chapter 7.

The PID Controller

The Hammerbot minimising the distance between the green and red cross using a PID controller. This is one of the cases from Experiment 7.3.2 in Chapter 7.

The Reality Gap

The Spinebot minimising the distance between the green and red cross using a steepest descent in Experiment 7.3.3 in Chapter 7. The real movemnt of a physical copy of the Spinebot can be seen next to the simulated movemnt.
